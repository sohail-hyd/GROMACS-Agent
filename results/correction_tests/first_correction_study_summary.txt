First correction-study summary

Tasks tested:
1. GSCF_001: pure water density at 300 K and 1 bar
2. GSCF_002: oxygen-oxygen RDF of pure water at 300 K
3. GSCF_003: water self-diffusion coefficient using MSD analysis

Baseline scores:
- GSCF_001: 9/10
- GSCF_002: 9/10
- GSCF_003: 8/10

Correction-loop results:
- GSCF_001 remained 9/10 after correction.
- GSCF_002 remained 9/10 after correction.
- GSCF_003 improved from 8/10 to 9/10 after a stricter second correction prompt.

Interpretation:
The correction loop improved the weaker MSD workflow but did not improve already-strong density and RDF workflows. This suggests that iterative evaluator-guided correction is useful, but its benefit depends on the severity and type of remaining workflow errors.

Main recurring issues:
1. Need for safe topology preparation.
2. Need for explicit non-interactive command handling.
3. Need for clear short-trajectory validation warnings.
4. Need for correct production ensemble choice for diffusion analysis.
5. Need for finite-size-effect warnings in small simulation boxes.

Status:
This completes the first prototype correction-study set. It is useful for the paper, but the dataset is still too small for a full publication.
